Tine grew up in Slovenia, a small country of only 2 million people. He obtained B.Sc. and M.Sc. in physics (summa cum laude), both from the University of Maribor, Slovenia. He joined the group of Prof. Daan Frenkel where he studied multivalent interactions and received his PhD from the University of Cambridge in November 2016. Later he had teaching (University of Maribor) and postdoc (Institute of Physics, Chinese academy of Sciences (IOP, CAS)) positions working on bioinformatics of genome detection and physics of DNA packing in viruses. He is a currently a Marie Curie postdoctoral fellow at Prof. Erik Luijten's research group at Northwestern University (since January 2019).

His expertise lies in statistical physics, physical chemistry, soft materials and computational methods, he also very much enjoys working with experimentalists.
